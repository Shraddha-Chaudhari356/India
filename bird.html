The Indian Peacock was declared the National Bird of India in 1963 due to its rich religious and cultural significance in Indian traditions. Known for its vibrant plumage and elegant dance, the peacock represents grace, beauty, and pride.

Peacocks are commonly found throughout the Indian subcontinent and are often associated with the monsoon season. They hold a prominent place in Indian mythology and are considered sacred in many cultures.
